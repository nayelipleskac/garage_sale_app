The Dragon's Blood Tree

A few facts about one of the world's most unusual trees...

The dragon blood tree has a unique and strange appearance, with an "upturned, densely packed crown having the shape of an uprightly held umbrella". This evergreen species is named after its dark red resin, which is known as "dragon's blood.

Where is this tree located?

Dracaena cinnabari, the Socotra dragon tree or dragon blood tree, is a dragon tree native to the Socotra archipelago, part of Yemen, located in the Arabian Sea.

Other important facts:

  • The dragon's blood tree usually produces its flowers around March, though flowering does vary with location. The flowers tend to grow at the end of the branches.
  • Dragon blood tree looks like giant mushroom thanks to its single trunk and umbrella-shaped crown.
  • Dragon blood tree blooms during February.
  • Injured bark and branches release red resin which is why this plant is known as dragon blood tree.
